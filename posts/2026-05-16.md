[POST TYPE B: Financial Education] - The difference between a Roth IRA and a traditional IRA - Audience: Married couples with adult children or empty nesters ages 45-60

Most people have heard of an IRA but never learned which one is actually right for them. 💡 Here's the simple truth: a **Traditional IRA** lets you deduct contributions now and pay taxes when you withdraw in retirement. A **Roth IRA** means you pay taxes now — but every dollar you pull out in retirement is completely tax-free. If you expect your income or tax rate to be higher later, a Roth is often the smarter move. The kids are grown. The nest is empty.
