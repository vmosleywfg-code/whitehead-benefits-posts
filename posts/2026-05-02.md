**Topic:** The SSA $255 Death Benefit Myth — What Social Security Actually Pays (And Why It's Not Enough)

---

⚠️ Have you seen those ads promising "up to $255 in federal burial benefits" for seniors? It sounds helpful — but there's a lot they're not telling you.

The Social Security lump-sum death payment of $255 is real, but it's a one-time payment that has been frozen at that amount since 1954. It's paid only to a surviving spouse or qualifying child — not to cover funeral costs, outstanding debts, or a family's ongoing financial needs.

The average funeral today costs between **$8,000 and $12,000**. That's before factoring in any medical bills, unpaid debts, or the financial burden left behind for a grieving family. $255 doesn't begin to touch that.

Those ads often use the words "federal" and "benefits" to make it sound like you're already covered — but what they're really selling is a lead-generation form. The truth is, the only way to truly protect your loved ones from those end-of-life costs is with a **private life insurance or final expense policy**. 💛

The good news: final expense life insurance for seniors is often more affordable than people expect, and it can be tailored to cover exactly what your family would need — no medical exam required in many cases. 🕊️
