# Life Insurance Post — May 2, 2026

**Topic:** Why Your Employer-Provided Life Insurance May Not Be Enough

---

💼 Your job offers life insurance — so you're covered, right? Not so fast.

Employer-provided life insurance is a wonderful benefit, but for most families, it's simply not enough on its own. Here's why:

**The coverage amount is usually too small.** Most group plans offer one to two times your annual salary. Financial experts typically recommend coverage equal to 10–12 times your income. If you earn $60,000 a year, your employer policy might pay out $60,000–$120,000 — which sounds like a lot, but can disappear quickly when you factor in a mortgage, childcare, outstanding debt, and years of lost income.

**You could lose it when you need it most.** Employer coverage is tied to your job. If you're laid off, change careers, or have to leave work due to illness, that policy disappears — often at exactly the moment your family's financial vulnerability is highest.

**You can't customize it.** Group plans are one-size-fits-all. They don't account for your family's unique needs, debts, or future goals. 🏠

The good news: a personal life insurance policy fills those gaps, travels with you wherever you work, and can be tailored to your exact situation. It's peace of mind that no employer can hand you — or take away.
