[POST TYPE B: Financial Education] - The True Cost of Minimum Payments on Credit Cards - Audience: Married couples with young children ages 28-45

You're juggling a mortgage, childcare, groceries, and a credit card bill that never seems to shrink. 💳 Sound familiar? Here's why: when you only pay the minimum, most of that payment goes straight to interest — not your actual balance. A $3,000 balance at 20% interest, paying only minimums, could take over 14 years to pay off and cost you more than $3,000 in interest alone. That's money that could have gone to your kids' college fund or a real family vacation. The minimum payment is designed to keep you paying — not to set you free. 💡 Understanding this one truth can completely change how your family handles debt.
