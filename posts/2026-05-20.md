[POST TYPE B: Financial Education] - How to build an emergency fund and why 3 to 6 months matters - Audience: Single parents, sole provider for children

As a single parent, you are the entire safety net for your kids. 🛡️ What happens if your car breaks down, you get sick, or you lose your job? That is where an emergency fund comes in. Financial experts recommend saving 3 to 6 months of living expenses in a separate account — money you do not touch unless it is a real emergency. Start small. Even $25 a week adds up to $1,300 in a year. The goal is to stop one unexpected expense from becoming a financial crisis. 💪 You work too hard to let life's surprises undo your progress.
