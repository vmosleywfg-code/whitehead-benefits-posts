[POST TYPE B: Financial Education] - What a beneficiary is and why updating it matters - Audience: Singles or couples ages 55-75, final expense and legacy planning

When was the last time you checked who gets your money? 💭 A beneficiary is the person you name to receive your life insurance, retirement accounts, or savings when you pass away. Here is the problem — many people set this once, years ago, and never looked back. An ex-spouse. A parent who has since passed. Someone you no longer even know. And here is what most people miss: your beneficiary designation overrides your will. Courts cannot fix it after the fact. ✅ Take 10 minutes today to review every policy and every account. Make sure the right people are protected.
